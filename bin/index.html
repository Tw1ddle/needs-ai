How It Works

This page uses a utility AI to control autonomous agents, in this case humans and zombies in the game world. Use the mouse to interact with the humans and zombies and watch the chaos unfold.

AI where NPCs consider their own needs leads to convincing awareness of their environments and autonomous behaviours without the need for brittle scripting. Utility-based AI works for many types of game.

The basic hierarchy is as follows: AI agents have Brains. Brains contain Reasoners. Reasoners contain ActionSets. ActionSets contain Considerations. Considerations (also known as Axes) hold Inputs, measurable aspects of the game world, and a way for an agent to consider (evaluate) the Inputs.

When it is time for an agent to think using their Brains, the available Actions are scored by evaluating their associated Considerations, and the best scoring actions are chosen through a strategy encapsulated by the ActionSets and Reasoners.

The challenging part of using a utility-based AI is the amount of design work and balancing needed to produce the behaviour you want. For example, an Input such as the distance from zombies must factor into human Considerations, but it may depend on the person.

Another challenging area is making Inputs available in a performant way, like how to represent generalized distances between objects, influence maps, elapsed times and other attributes.

This AI demo was written using Haxe. If you have any suggestions, requests or comments then contact me or open an issue.
